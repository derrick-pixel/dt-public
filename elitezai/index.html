Conditional Average Treatment Effect (CATE)

? CATE = E[Y(1) - Y(0) | X=x]. Unlike the ATE which averages effects across all customers, CATE estimates the treatment effect for specific sub-groups (industry, size, tech maturity), enabling precision targeting of high-response segments.

Identifies which SME segments respond most strongly to each workflow deployment

How We Use CATE in Practice
① We estimate the treatment effect of deploying each workflow (e.g., HR Contract Automation) across sub-groups defined by industry, headcount band, and current tech stack.
② Segments with CATE > 2σ above the ATE are prioritised in sales outreach — these companies see the highest ROI and generate the strongest referrals.
③ Key finding: Professional Services SMEs (50–100 headcount, Xero-integrated) show a CATE that is 3.2× the average treatment effect, making them our highest-value acquisition target.
🔬

Complier Average Treatment Effect (CATE / LATE)

? LATE uses an instrumental variable (IV) to identify causal effects when not all treated units comply. Our IV is PSG grant eligibility — eligible SMEs are more likely to adopt. LATE = ITT ÷ First-Stage (compliance rate), isolating the causal effect on compliers.

Why This Matters
Simple before/after comparisons overstate ROI because early adopters are self-selected high performers. LATE gives us a clean causal estimate — the productivity gain attributable solely to the workflow, not to the type of firm that chose to adopt it.
