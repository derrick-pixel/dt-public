§ III — Where flashcart fits

The bundle does what none of the five do.

Onsite + brand-funded + reportable + experiential + replaces multiple lines. None of the five archetypes fill this gap as a single buy.

vs Big SI / Pico
Additive, not competitive
Pico runs the production. Flashcart shows up at the event Pico produces, brings the inventory rail and the checkout. We do not own AV/stage; we don't try to.
Sells alongside Pico, not against — different jobs.
vs voucher platform (Giftano)
Live retail experience, not a post-event email
Giftano vouchers ship after the event — utility-grade. Flashcart is the live moment: staff browse the catalogue at the event, pick what they want, walk out with the SKU. Higher emotional bandwidth.
Sells against the "post-event voucher" reflex.
vs photo booth
More engagement minutes per attendee
A photo booth is 90 seconds of attention per attendee. A flashcart pop-up holds attention for 5–15 minutes. More engagement minutes = higher post-event NPS at the same dollar spend.
Sells as a higher-engagement-per-dollar replacement.
vs DIY (HR procures lucky-draw inventory)
Brand-sample pricing beats Lazada bulk
HR doing cold lucky-draw procurement on Lazada/Carousell pays retail-minus-bulk. Flashcart catalogue runs at brand-sample / surplus pricing — 40–70% under retail. Same prize pool dollars buy 2–3× the perceived value.
Sells as a procurement upgrade.
vs assembled DIY stack
One contract replaces three
DIY stack is voucher contract + booth contract + lucky-draw procurement (3 contracts, 3 invoices, 3 reporting decks). Flashcart Annual Engagement Partner is one contract, one invoice, one reporting deck. Procurement-load reduction is itself a sale.
Sells as MNC procurement-load reduction.

§ VI — Pre-empted objections

The pushback HR will raise.

"Our D&D budget is locked already this year."

Flashcart fits inside the existing lucky-draw + goodie-bag + booth lines — replacing them, not adding to total. Net-zero or net-negative on D&D budget when sized correctly.

"How is brand-funded inventory not just hidden marketing?"

Transparent at the event: SKUs are at brand-sample pricing because the brand pays for the sampling reach. Staff understand the trade — they get the SKU at 40–70% under retail; the brand gets attention. Better than hidden vendor markups.

"What if my MNC HQ doesn't allow on-premises commerce?"

Some MNCs have policy restrictions on on-premises commerce. We can run flashcart at the event venue (hotel ballroom for D&D, function space for family day) instead of inside the office. Coverage drops but the model still works.

"Will brands actually want to fund this?"

FMCG and beauty brands routinely fund SGD 5–25k events for SGD 8–15/unit sampling reach. Flashcart is on-the-bullseye for SG MNC office workers who are too time-poor for trade fairs. Founding-partner programmes lock 3 brands Year-1.

"How do I justify this at the post-event review?"

Post-event reporting deck: attendance, GMV, NPS, brand-sample reach. The same metrics MNC HR HQ already tracks for engagement events — just measurable for the first time across all three lines (voucher / booth / lucky draw).

"What if the inventory is wrong for our crowd?"

Pre-event audience profiling — staff demographic, cultural mix, common spend patterns. We curate the SKU drop accordingly. If the curation misses, Year-2 catalogue gets adjusted; refund mechanics on the rework guarantee handle the edge case.
