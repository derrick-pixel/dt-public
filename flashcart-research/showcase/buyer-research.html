§ II — The HR Engagement Lead

Attendance-driven. NPS-anxious. Procurement-fatigued.

The MNC HR Engagement Lead sits in a specific psychological territory: their KPI is attendance and post-event NPS, not vendor revenue. They've been measured on engagement-program impact for three+ years now and the metrics are flat. Their vendor stack is procurement-heavy (4–6 weeks pre-event), feels arbitrary, and they have no reliable way to demonstrate ROI to MNC HQ.

Demographics
SG-based MNC, 500–5,000 staff
Title varies: HR Engagement Lead, EX Lead, People Experience Manager, Culture & Engagement Director. Reports up to CHRO or APAC HR. Manages annual D&D + town hall + family day + perks days as the core program.
The bullseye: 4,500 SG MNCs / large SMEs.
Annual engagement budget
SGD 50–150k / yr (engagement events line)
Per Get Out! Events 2026: D&D total SGD 100–180/head all-in; family day SGD 30–80/head; town hall SGD 50–150/head; lucky draw SGD 500–2,000/event; goodie bag SGD 3–80/item. Mid-MNC of 1,000 staff: SGD 75–125k/yr engagement-events line.
Annual Engagement Partner at SGD 12,000 lands inside 15% of this envelope.
Vendor-line count
4–6 vendors per program
Production agency (Pico/INX) + voucher platform (Giftano) + photo booth (Vivid Snaps) + lucky-draw inventory (DIY) + caterer + AV. Procurement onboards each separately. 4–6 weeks pre-event of vendor wrangling.
Vendor consolidation is itself a sale. Less procurement load = less HR overhead.
KPI tension
Attendance + NPS, not vendor revenue
MNC HR HQ measures the Engagement Lead on attendance and post-event NPS. Lucky-draw budget feels arbitrary; goodie bags rarely land in employees' top recall. The buyer is desperate for a measurable line — one they can carry to the HR-program review.
Sell the post-event reporting deck as much as the activation itself.
Hybrid-work shift
80% of SG employers are hybrid
Per Get Out! Events 2026 trends: 80%+ of SG employers run hybrid. HR is shifting budget from 1–2 mega-events to a cadence of smaller activations. Quarterly tier sits on this trend; the Annual tier captures the full cadence.
Pitch quarterly tier as "the cadence model HR is moving to anyway."
Procurement onboarding
Standard MNC vendor record + PCI-DSS
MNC procurement requires standard vendor onboarding: NDA, PCI-DSS payment compliance, KYC of the entity, SLA documentation. Replacing 4 vendor records with 1 is itself a headline number for procurement teams' KPIs.
Talk to procurement, not just HR. Procurement-load reduction is a parallel sale.

§ V — Decision-making process

Six-week procurement, two-meeting decision.

The MNC HR Engagement Lead's actual buying process. Understanding this shapes the sales motion: scoping call, scoping note, post-event pilot, contract.

Trigger
"Annual D&D vendor RFQ kicks off in March"
SG MNC budget cycles run April–March. Engagement Lead opens RFQ for the next fiscal year's D&D in February–March. Quarterly micro-activations get added later as separate scopes.
Sales lifecycle is calendar-aligned. Pitch by Feb–March.
First meeting
Scoping call (20–30 min)
Engagement Lead walks the calendar (D&D, family day, town hall, perks). Flashcart confirms the catalogue mix, brand-sponsor coverage, and which event line gets replaced first. Output: scoping note pdf.
Single Event tier as a low-friction pilot pre-contract.
Pilot event
Single Event tier (free to host)
Brand-funded inventory, no host commitment. Engagement Lead gets to see the pop-up live, pull the GMV+attendance+NPS report, walk the numbers to procurement.
Pilot is the closer. After one good Single Event, the Annual contract is procurement-paperwork.
Procurement onboarding
Standard MNC vendor record (2–3 weeks)
NDA + KYC + PCI-DSS docs + SLA + reporting commitment. Replaces 4 prior vendor records. Onboarding is parallel to first paid event.
Have the vendor onboarding pack ready Day 1 of pilot.
Post-event review
Reporting deck → MNC HQ HR review
Quarterly attendance + NPS + GMV + brand-sample reach deck. Engagement Lead shares with APAC HR + global HR HQ. Becomes the engagement-program ROI line they didn't have before.
The reporting deck is the renewal lever. Year-2 contract is decided here.
§ VI — Implications for pricing

What the buyer research tells the price card.

Anchor MNC pricing on attendance + NPS, not revenue share

Engagement Lead is judged on attendance and post-event NPS, not vendor revenue. The Annual contract should report attendance-uplift metrics — never a GMV split with the host.

Wellness-fair WHG-claimable structure for SMEs

SG Workplace Health Grant covers wellness-fair structures up to 50% of cost. Quarterly tier deliberately lands inside the WHG envelope for a near-S$1,250 effective post-PSG quarterly cost.

Free at the entry, brand-funded

Single Event tier removes host friction entirely — brands pay for sampling reach. Hosts pilot at zero cost and decide whether to graduate. This is the funnel-top model the rest of the tier ladder relies on.

Year-1 capture concentrated on MNCs (≥70%)

MNC penetration of structured engagement events is much higher than SME. 70%+ of Year-1 capture should come from MNCs at Annual Engagement Partner contracts. SME activations are volume-seeding, not revenue-load-bearing.

Procurement-load reduction is a parallel sale

Replacing 4 vendor records with 1 is itself a headline procurement KPI. Procurement teams hate vendor sprawl. Pitch the procurement-load reduction in parallel with the engagement-uplift sale.

Brand-sponsor pricing decoupled from host pricing

Brand sponsors fund inventory under separate contracts. Host tiers stay clean (host pays for activation + reporting, brand pays for sampling reach). Decoupling the two contracts simplifies the host conversation.
