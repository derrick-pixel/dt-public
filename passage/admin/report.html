    Section 03

    Executive Summary

    Five anchor findings. Each is backed by the data appendix that follows in Sections 4–10.

    1. The Singapore casket market is structurally opaque. Roughly ~24,000 deaths a year; about ~65% take the wake-and-embalm path bundled into S$8–15K full packages, and ~35% take direct cremation. Inside those packages the casket alone is marked up to S$2,000–S$4,000 — never itemised, by design.
    2. Why Passage wins. All-in transparent S$499 / S$999 / S$1,299 casket-only SKUs vs hidden S$2–4K parlour markup. 3-hour island-wide delivery with 1-hour emergency surge. FSC Chain of Custody, 60% local timber, <5% material waste. Binding 30% of net profit to charity (FY2024 S$48,200 across Singapore Hospice Council, SASCO, Metta Welfare). 47 free caskets given FY2024 (31 social-worker referrals + 16 family applications). Tripartite triple-signatory + Progressive Wage Mark.
    3. Top three white-space niches. (1) Anti-Package Consumer Education — "The S$600 Casket Receipt" aimed at ~5,500 cost-conscious / anti-pressure households per year. (2) Hospital Social-Worker Partnership — free-casket discharge-fast pathway, 200–400 addressable cases/year as referrals compound. (3) Eco / Pre-Arrangement for the FSC-Aware Senior Planner — ~5,700 households of 55–75-year-olds who weight ESG in legacy planning.
    4. Design audit reality. Industry average 6.3 across six SG players on a 5-dimension rubric; Passage scores 6.4, third behind Ang Chin Moh (7.4) and 1Last (7.0). Gap to leader is 1.0 point — almost entirely on visual brand distinctiveness and surfaced credibility proofs. The fix is to surface what already exists: 30% charity meter on the hero, free-casket counter, audited ESG/charity PDF.
    5. Five recommended actions. Surface the 30% charity meter on the homepage hero. Ship an audited FY2024 ESG/Charity PDF download. Build persona-led landing pages for the five ICPs. Live free-casket counter on the hero (47 FY2024). Hospital social-worker discharge-fast pilot with on-call coordinator paging.

        Section 09

        Recommendations

        Five deck-grounded action items. Each one connects a real Passage proof point already on the public site to the place a buyer is most likely to decide.

        1. Surface the 30% charity meter on the homepage hero
          Move the binding 30% net profit to charity commitment and the FY2024 S$48,200 across Singapore Hospice Council, SASCO, Metta Welfare totals into the hero zone of index.html and into a persistent strip at the top of every product page. Today this lives only on accreditation.html; the buyer is not reading it where they decide.
        2. Ship an audited FY2024 ESG / Charity PDF download
          Add a "Download FY2024 ESG & Charity Report (PDF)" CTA in the accreditation page header and the product-card footer. Quote the binding constitution clause inside. Convert the 4.2 t CO₂e per-casket figure, 60% local timber, FSC Chain of Custody ID, and Tripartite signatures into a single defensible artefact a journalist or a procurement officer can email around.
        3. Persona-led landing pages for the five ICPs
          Five distinct entry points: Cost-Conscious Bereaved, Anti-Pressure Consumer, Eco / Values Buyer, Pre-Arrangement Senior, Social-Worker Referral. Each with its own hero copy, its own CTA, and its own proof stack — the cost-conscious page leads with the S$2-4K markup comparison; the social-worker page leads with the FY2024 47-casket programme, the +65 9847 9776 helpline and an eligibility checklist.
        4. Live free-casket counter widget on the hero
          A counter that reads "47 free caskets given in FY2024 — and counting" on the homepage hero, updating live as families are accepted into the programme. Pulls from the same data source the audited charity ledger uses, so the counter and the report cannot drift out of sync.
        5. Hospital social-worker pilot — discharge-fast pathway
          Direct outreach to SGH / NUH / TTSH / KKH bereavement social workers, MOH-funded nursing-home discharge planners, AIC eldercare and Singapore Hospice Council member teams. One number, one form, one dignified casket within hours. Eligibility checklist printed and PDF, response-time SLA, on-call coordinator paging, surge fee waived for verified welfare-org partners. SASCO and Metta as charity-partner co-branded referral cards.
