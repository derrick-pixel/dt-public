## 6. ICPs / target buyer segments (inferred from pages + accreditation)

1. **Cost-conscious bereaved families** — primary. Recently-bereaved Singaporeans facing opaque all-in funeral packages, want transparent S$499-1,200 casket and the rest a-la-carte.
2. **Eco-conscious / values-driven buyers** — care about FSC timber, local assembly, 60% EV by 2026.
3. **Anti-pressure / consumer-rights advocates** — refuse to be pressured by funeral directors, want to challenge casket markups.
4. **Pre-arrangement / senior planners** — living seniors planning ahead, want fixed-price certainty.
5. **Social-impact buyers** — buy because 30% goes to charity and free-casket programme is a real obligation, not a marketing line.
6. **ComCare / social-worker-referred** — free casket recipients (deceased without next-of-kin, means-tested welfare).

## 7. What Passage is NOT (critical to keep agents honest)

- **NOT a full funeral services platform** — does not sell wakes, embalming, hearses, cremation, columbarium, rites, livestream, post-funeral admin, grief counselling, CPF claims, or estate administration.
- **NOT a digital bereavement platform** with end-to-end services. Just caskets + supporting delivery.
- **No religious-rites packages**. The `knowledge.html` Funeral Guide is informational educational content, not a service offering.
- **No livestream / memorial page** as a product.
- **No same-day wake setup** (the closest service is 1-hour emergency casket delivery via +S$150 surcharge).
- The `faq.html` is consumer-rights information, not a customer-success service.

## 8. Real direct competitors (the casket-cost-hiding incumbents)
