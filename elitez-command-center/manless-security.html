The 2030 Mandate

Compliance is no longer optional.

Under the Foreign Employee Dormitories Act (FEDA), every facility housing seven or more workers must hold an operating licence. The Dormitory Transition Scheme (DTS) requires roughly 1,000 Factory-Converted and Purpose-Built Dormitories to retrofit improved security and public-health standards by 31 December 2030. FCD Security delivers those controls as a managed service — no in-house security team to build.

The Connected Dormitory

Every control on the operator's console

FCD Security only works if the command centre can act, not just watch. The IoT control plane turns the dormitory's devices into live controls — the biometric gantries, the lighting, the perimeter, the plant room — so an operator answers an event in seconds, and scheduled virtual patrols walk the blocks with no officer on the ground.

Perimeter & rooftop sensing

Fence-line and roof-access sensors flag intrusion and line-crossing the moment it happens — after-hours cover with no patrol gap.

Common-area smart lighting

Corridor and stairwell lighting on motion and schedule — and lit up on demand as a deterrent when an alert is verified.

Gate & turnstile control

Remote release, hold or lockdown of the biometric gantries and vehicle gates — including a one-action facility lockdown.

Tank, leak & safety sensors

Water-tank level, plant-room leak and smoke sensors raise a tracked event — covering the DTS public-health resilience standard.
