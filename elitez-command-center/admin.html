The Bottom Line

What the intel says — in four findings

Finding 01

The integrated-mid-market slot is empty

Integrators that can bundle (Certis, ISS, AETOS) price for government scale; the affordable security-tech specialists (Oneberry, Gabkotech) are single-service. Nobody owns command-centre-coordinated IFM at mid-market pricing.

Finding 02

Regulation is the demand engine

A legally-fixed PWM wage escalator through 2028, mandatory government Outcome-Based Contracting, and the FEDA/DTS-2030 dormitory retrofit window all push buyers toward a tech-led model. The market has already accepted the problem.

Finding 03

The dormitory wedge is sharp and time-boxed

~1,000 FEDA dormitories are forced into a retrofit cycle closing in 2030, with the DTS grant window open now. This is attack-plan #1 — priced below a post-2026 guard roster.

Finding 04

It is a margin/share play, not a land-grab

The market grows only ~3% a year. Revenue comes from winning contracts off body-shop incumbents on delivery-model differentiation — a realistic ~S$11–12M ARR by year 3.
