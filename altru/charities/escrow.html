Escrow & Fund Flow — Paid in Gross.

How money moves from a wedding guest's PayNow tap to your charity's UEN — and why the structure satisfies §B.1 of the Code for Commercial Fundraisers. Written for finance teams.

The "Paid in Gross" Model

Altru's fund flow is structured to comply with §B.1 of the Code for Commercial Fundraisers: "Commercial Fund-raisers must hand over all donations collected directly to the Charity without any deduction or set-off."

💍
Wedding guest

Initiates a gift via PayNow on the couple's Altru page.

🏦
Altru escrow

Charity portion held briefly during the 14-day couple-authorisation window.

🎗️
Your charity UEN

100% of the charity portion remitted in gross via direct PayNow-UEN transfer.

🧾
Altru invoice

Separate monthly service invoice for the 5% platform fee.

What this means in practice

  • The charity portion of every gift is remitted to your UEN at 100%, with no deduction.
  • Your books record the full gross amount as charitable income — clean for IRAS, clean for your annual return, clean for your auditors.
  • The 5% platform fee is invoiced separately, monthly, paid from your own operating funds. It is a service fee, not a fundraising deduction.
  • This model is consistent with how reputable commercial fundraisers in Singapore operate, and with the Commissioner of Charities' guidance.

Why a 5% platform fee

  • Singapore's Charities Act caps commercial fundraiser fees at 30% of gross receipts (the "30/70 rule").
  • Industry typical: 20–30% on commission, often more for telemarketing or street programmes.
  • Altru's 5% covers technology, IRAS-receipt support, escrow operations, and PDPA compliance only. We are intentionally well below the cap.
  • Because we are not commission-driven, we have no incentive to push donors towards larger or unwanted gifts — the agency problem the 30/70 rule was written to prevent.
For your finance team. Each month you receive (a) a remittance statement showing every charity disbursement to your UEN, with the wedding/event reference and donor count; and (b) a separate Altru tax invoice for that month's 5% service fee. The two flows are structurally independent and can be reconciled cleanly to your bank statements.

The 14-Day Authorisation Window

The brief escrow period exists to give wedding couples a defined window to confirm gifts received against their actual ang-bao tally. After 14 days the system either disburses or auto-refunds — there is no indefinite hold.

1

Guest sends gift via PayNow

Charity portion enters Altru's segregated escrow account, held under MAS Payment Services Act 2019 oversight. Personal portion routes to the couple's nominated account.

2

State: pending — clock starts

The 14-day countdown begins from the timestamp of the gift. The couple is notified via their dashboard and email; they can review every gift, confirm matches, and resolve any discrepancies with the guest directly.

3

Couple authorises → state: authorized

On authorisation, 100% of the charity portion is released to your PayNow-UEN within one banking day. You receive the disbursement notification with the gift reference.

4

No authorisation in 14 days → state: auto_refunded

If the couple does not authorise within the window, the platform automatically refunds the guest in full, end-to-end. Your charity is never affected by an incomplete authorisation — you only ever see successful, authorised gifts.

5

Guest-initiated refund within 14 days → state: refunded

Guests retain a unilateral right to cancel during the window (e.g., if the gift was sent in error). This drops to zero after release.

Segregation. The escrow account holds donor funds only — it is never commingled with Altru's operating revenue, and sub-ledger reconciliation is performed daily. Audit trails are available to your finance team and to any regulator on request.
Why 14 days. Long enough for a couple returning from a honeymoon to reconcile against their physical ang-bao envelopes; short enough that funds cannot be staged indefinitely. Same window we use for breach-cure under PFA cl.7.1(b).

Monthly Reconciliation — What Your Finance Team Receives

Two distinct artefacts, by the 7th working day of each month. Both reconcile cleanly to your bank statements.

1 · Remittance statement

A line-by-line record of every charity-portion disbursement to your PayNow-UEN in the preceding calendar month.

  • Wedding / event reference
  • Named donor (the couple) and NRIC for IRAS-receipt purposes
  • Gross gift amount
  • PayNow transaction reference and timestamp
  • State at disbursement (always authorized)

Format: CSV and PDF. Same fields every month; ingestion-friendly for any standard accounting system.

2 · Service-fee tax invoice

A separate Altru tax invoice for the 5% service fee on the same month's gross disbursements. GST inclusive (where applicable). Payable on standard 30-day terms.

  • Itemised by wedding / event reference
  • Cross-references the remittance statement
  • Standard tax-invoice fields (UEN, GST number where applicable, ABR)

The invoice is for a service provided to your charity — separate from, and structurally independent of, the donation flow.

Ad-hoc extracts. Your finance team or appointed auditor may request platform extracts at any reasonable time, with same-business-day turnaround under PFA cl.6A. Records are retained for 5 years from the end of the relevant financial year.

How the Fund Flow Maps to Singapore Law

Code for Commercial Fundraisers §B.1

"Commercial Fund-raisers must hand over all donations collected directly to the Charity without any deduction or set-off." → Altru remits 100% to your UEN; the fee is a separate invoice.

PFA cl.2.3 — direct routing

"Donations [are] paid directly to CHARITY's designated bank account." → Charity portion is sent to your designated PayNow-UEN at the point of release.

Payment Services Act 2019 (MAS)

The escrow account holds customer funds segregated from operating funds, with daily sub-ledger reconciliation. MAS-supervised payments infrastructure.

30/70 efficiency rule

Aggregate fundraising expenses must remain below 30% of gross receipts. Altru reports its 5% line annually so your charity can confirm the consolidated ratio.

Fund-Raising Regulations

No cash collected. PayNow-only flow. Refund right and 5-year record retention all built into the platform.

Tax — Income Tax Act, Part XII

Donors of record (the couple) receive IRAS-eligible receipts from your charity at the standard 250% deduction rate, supported by Altru's remittance metadata.
