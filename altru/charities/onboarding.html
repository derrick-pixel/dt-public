How Altru Raises Funds for Your Charity

Altru is a wedding-giving platform. Couples invite guests to direct a portion of their ang-bao gift to one of three curated IPC partner charities. Your charity receives funds in gross, on the same banking rail your other donations use.

The wedding context

A couple onboards Altru in the months before their wedding and selects one or more partner charities. Wedding guests, invited by the couple, send their ang-bao gift through Altru using PayNow. The guest splits the gift between the couple's personal portion and the charitable portion, with a default split suggested by the couple.

The practical effect for your charity: a new digital channel — designed around an existing Singapore custom — that introduces your cause to a steady stream of guests who would not otherwise be on a charity mailing list, in the most positive setting imaginable.

Why this is good for charities

  • Audience expansion. Wedding guests are typically aged 25–55, give in groups, and are usually not on existing charity acquisition lists.
  • Positive context. The donation is part of a celebration — far higher emotional resonance than cold solicitation.
  • Zero acquisition cost. You do not pay for marketing; you pay only the platform fee, on funds successfully delivered to your UEN.
  • No reputational entanglement. Your charity is presented as one of three IPC partners — never as the charity "of" any specific wedding.
  • Tax-relief eligible. All gifts qualify for the standard 250% tax deduction at the donor's IRAS filing.
What we are not. Altru is not a charity, not a donor-advised fund, and not a custodian of donated funds. We are a commercial platform regulated as a third-party fundraiser under the Charities Act and the Code for Commercial Fundraisers issued by the Commissioner of Charities. All donor-facing materials disclose this clearly.

The 30/70 Rule

Total fundraising expenses (across all commercial fundraisers and direct campaigns) must remain below 30% of your charity's gross fundraising receipts in any financial year. Altru's 5% is well inside that envelope.

"Paid in Gross"

Code for Commercial Fundraisers §B.1 requires that fundraisers hand over donations to the charity without deduction. Altru's flow implements this exactly — see Escrow & Fund Flow.

IPC Status & the 250% Deduction

An IPC's official receipt entitles the donor to a 250% deduction at IRAS. Altru passes the donor's NRIC and consent through to your charity solely so you can issue this receipt.

5-Year Record Retention

Both the MOA and the PFA templates require fundraising records to be retained for at least 5 years from the end of the relevant financial year. Altru maintains this internally and provides extracts on request.

The Two Standard Agreements — MOA vs PFA

Singapore charities typically use one of two template agreements when engaging an external fundraiser. Altru is willing to operate under either; the Professional Fundraiser Agreement (PFA) is the more common fit for a digital commercial platform.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does onboarding take, end-to-end?

Typically two to three weeks from a complete application. The bulk of the time is the joint walk-through call, the legal review of the PFA on your side, and the brand-kit and content-template approval. Technical activation is same-week once signed.

Is there an onboarding fee?

No. Application is free and onboarding is free. The 5% platform fee is the only charge, and only on funds successfully delivered to your UEN.

Can we cap or suspend our participation?

Yes — at any time, for any reason, with two months' notice. We stop new gift designations to your charity once notified, and clear the in-flight escrow over the next 14 days under the standard authorisation flow.

What if a wedding turns out to be problematic — say, the marriage is annulled or there is a public incident?

Altru retains the right to pause disbursement of any specific event's escrow and to consult with you before proceeding. In practice, the 14-day window means most concerns surface inside the auto-refund period, and the platform fails safe — to the guest, not to your charity.

Can our charity see who gave?

You see the named donor of record (the couple) for IRAS-receipt purposes. You do not see the individual wedding guests' names or contact details. See PDPA Programme for the full picture.

How do we issue tax-deduction receipts?

Altru passes you the named donor's NRIC and the gift amount on a monthly schedule, alongside the disbursement statement. Your charity issues the IRAS receipt to the named donor in your usual cycle.

What happens to the 5% if a gift is refunded?

Refunds are pre-authorisation, so no fee has been incurred — there is nothing to reverse. The fee applies only to gifts successfully released to your UEN.

Are we covered if Altru has a data breach?

Yes. Altru indemnifies your charity for breaches caused by Altru's systems or staff, and carries cyber-liability and professional-indemnity insurance covering this exposure.

How does this interact with the Commissioner of Charities and our annual return?

Altru's 5% is a fundraising expense to be aggregated with your other fundraising costs in your annual return. We provide supporting figures by your financial year-end, in the format your auditors are used to.

Can we propose changes to the indicative terms?

Of course. The indicative terms are our opening position, not a take-it-or-leave-it. We accommodate reasonable adjustments — particularly on data, complaints, and reporting cadence.

Is Altru itself a charity?

No. Altru is a private commercial company registered in Singapore, operating as a third-party fundraiser under the Charities Act and the Code for Commercial Fundraisers. Every donor-facing surface discloses this clearly.
