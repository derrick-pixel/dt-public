PDPA Programme — Data Minimisation by Design.

Altru is built so that your charity is exposed only to the data it actually needs — the named donor of record, for IRAS-receipt purposes. Wedding-guest data stays inside the platform. This page is written for charity DPOs and retained counsel.

Data Minimisation — Collection & Visibility

We collect only what is required to operate a gift, issue a tax-deductible receipt, and meet our regulatory obligations — and we contain that data inside Singapore.

What Altru collects

  • Donor (wedding guest): name, mobile number, gift amount, optional NRIC for tax-receipt purposes only, optional message to the couple.
  • Couple: names, NRIC of one or both partners (for IRAS receipt where the couple are the named donors), wedding date, contact details.
  • Charity: registered name, UEN, IPC No., authorised contact, finance/PDPA officer.
  • Operational metadata: timestamps, transaction references, audit-trail entries.

No location data. No device fingerprinting. No third-party advertising trackers.

What your charity sees

  • Aggregate disbursement amount per gift / per wedding event.
  • Receipt-issuance fields for the named donor only — i.e., the couple's NRIC and the couple's name (this is the donor of record for IRAS purposes).
  • Reconciliation references (transaction ID, Altru event reference).

Your charity does not see the names, contact details, or NRICs of individual wedding guests. They are not your donors of record under the IRAS receipt; the named donor is the couple.

The principle. Your charity's PDPA exposure is reduced to the minimum required to issue a valid tax receipt. The privacy attack surface for your organisation — the data you have to protect, audit, and account for — is held to the contents of one receipt per couple, not one record per guest.

PDPA Programme — Clause by Clause

Mapped to the obligations of the Personal Data Protection Act 2012 and the appendix-D structure used by both the PFA and MOA templates.

PDPA ObligationHow Altru complies
Consent & purpose limitation Every donor explicitly consents at the point of giving, with a clear statement that personal data is used only for gift processing, IRAS receipts, and the operation of the platform. No marketing use. No profiling.
Notification The privacy notice is presented before submission, not after. Plain-English summary above the fold; full notice one click away.
Access & correction Donors and couples can request access to and correction of their data within 30 days, by writing to dpo@altru.asia. Charity-facing requests routed jointly with you under the PFA.
Retention Personal data retained for the legal minimum (5 years from the end of the relevant financial year) for receipts and audit; deleted thereafter, unless the donor has consented to longer retention or a regulator requires it.
Transfer outside Singapore None. All Altru personal data is stored on infrastructure resident in Singapore.
Data Breach Notification (Part VIA) Altru notifies the PDPC and your charity within 72 hours of confirmed assessment of a notifiable breach, with full particulars and remediation steps. Joint response coordinated under PFA App.D ¶4.
Protection (security) Encryption in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest, principle-of-least-privilege access controls, immutable audit logs, and quarterly penetration testing of the platform.
Accountability (DPO) Altru maintains a Data Protection Officer; your charity nominates yours on the application form. Quarterly joint review of incidents and access requests.
Indemnity Altru indemnifies your charity for breaches caused by Altru's systems or staff (PFA cl.6.2 / MOA App.). Altru carries cyber-liability and professional-indemnity insurance covering this exposure.

Breach Notification & Joint Response

PDPA Part VIA requires notifiable breaches to be reported to the PDPC within prescribed timeframes. Altru's programme is designed to discharge this obligation while keeping your charity informed and co-operative.

Notification flow

  1. Altru detects or is alerted to a suspected breach.
  2. Initial assessment within 30 days (PDPC requirement).
  3. If confirmed notifiable, Altru notifies the PDPC within 3 calendar days and your charity within 72 hours.
  4. Joint communication plan agreed if affected individuals must be notified.
  5. Post-incident review and remediation report shared with your DPO.

Your charity's role

  • Receive Altru's notification through your nominated DPO.
  • Co-operate on the joint response — disclosure wording, affected-individual list, regulator communications.
  • You are not liable for Altru-caused breaches; the indemnity in PFA cl.6.2 / MOA App. is structural, not aspirational.
  • You retain the right to make your own regulator filings if you consider them necessary.
Quarterly joint review. Altru's DPO and your DPO meet quarterly to review: (a) all access / correction requests; (b) any near-miss incidents not rising to notifiable level; (c) changes in regulator guidance; and (d) any consent or retention questions raised by either side. Minutes shared and retained.

Technical & Organisational Safeguards

Infrastructure

  • Compute and data storage on Singapore-resident infrastructure (Cloudflare Singapore edge for static; Singapore-bound origin storage).
  • TLS 1.3 in transit; AES-256 at rest.
  • Daily encrypted backups, 35-day retention, restore-tested quarterly.
  • Immutable audit log of all access to PII.

Access & identity

  • Principle of least privilege; access requires explicit grant and a documented business purpose.
  • MFA required for all production access.
  • Quarterly access review with revocation of stale grants.
  • No vendor or contractor access to production PII without your DPO's prior approval.

Testing & assurance

  • Quarterly third-party penetration testing; reports shared on request under NDA.
  • Continuous secret-scanning and dependency monitoring in CI/CD.
  • Annual tabletop exercise of the breach-response runbook.

People

  • Background checks on all personnel with production access.
  • Mandatory PDPA training for all staff, refreshed annually.
  • Confidentiality and IP assignment in every employment / contractor agreement.
  • Documented offboarding revokes access within one business day.
Bottom line for your DPO. Your charity is exposed only to the named donor's IRAS-receipt data — exactly what the receipt itself contains. Wedding-guest data stays inside Altru's platform, used solely to operate the gift and refund flows. There is no cross-border transfer; there is no marketing list; there is no shared analytics environment.
