Privacy Notice

Effective date: 11 May 2026 · Controller: Altru Asia Pte Ltd (Singapore) · DPO contact: dpo@altru.asia

Altru Asia Pte Ltd ("Altru", "we", "us") is a Singapore commercial third-party fundraiser. We operate altru.asia, a platform that lets wedding guests direct a portion of their ang-bao gift to an IPC-registered Singapore charity chosen by the couple. This Privacy Notice sets out what personal data we collect, why, how long we keep it, and the rights you have under Singapore's Personal Data Protection Act 2012 (PDPA).

1 · Who this notice covers

This notice applies to four groups of individuals whose personal data Altru processes:

  • Wedding guests sending a gift through Altru.
  • Couples registering a wedding on Altru.
  • Authorised representatives of partner charities and prospective partner charities.
  • Visitors to altru.asia who interact with our site but do not transact.

We do not collect location data, biometric data, or device fingerprints. We do not run third-party advertising trackers or cross-site analytics on altru.asia.

3 · Lawful basis — consent and purpose limitation

Under the PDPA, our processing rests on the consent you give us when you provide your data on this site. That consent is given to a clearly stated purpose, and we will not repurpose your data:

  • We use personal data only for the purposes set out in Section 2.
  • We do not use it for marketing, profiling, or product development.
  • We do not sell, rent, or licence it to third parties.

4 · Who we share data with

Personal data is disclosed only as follows:

  • Partner charities receive the named donor's NRIC and gift amount where the donor has requested an IRAS tax receipt — the minimum required to issue that receipt. Wedding-guest contact details are not shared with charities.
  • Payment service providers (HitPay Payment Solutions Pte Ltd or any successor) process the gift transaction and refunds. They act as independent controllers for their own purposes (KYC, anti-money-laundering, MAS reporting) and as our processor for the transaction itself.
  • Government authorities (PDPC, MAS, Commissioner of Charities, IRAS, Singapore Police Force) where we are legally required to respond or where a regulator has issued a valid request.
  • Professional advisers (auditors, lawyers, insurers) under contractual confidentiality, where strictly necessary.

5 · International transfers

Altru's primary systems are operated on infrastructure within Singapore (Cloudflare's Singapore edge for the site and serverless compute; Cloudflare D1 for primary data, with the Singapore region selected at provisioning). Where a payment processor or other vendor processes data outside Singapore in the ordinary course of providing its service, we ensure standard of protection comparable to the PDPA via contractual safeguards.

6 · Retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary for the purpose stated, with a backstop minimum and a hard maximum:

  • Statutory minimum: 5 years from the end of the financial year to which the record relates, as required by the Charities Act and Income Tax Act record-retention obligations.
  • Operational minimum: for active couples and gifts in flight, for the duration of the relationship plus the 14-day authorisation window.
  • Maximum: 7 years from the end of the relationship, unless a regulator requires longer or you have given separate consent to longer retention.
  • Authentication tokens: 15 minutes for one-time magic-link tokens; logged-in session tokens, 30 days from last use.

After the maximum retention period elapses, personal data is irreversibly deleted or anonymised.

7 · Your rights under the PDPA

You may, at any time:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you and request information on how it has been used or disclosed in the past year.
  • Correct any personal data we hold that is inaccurate or out of date.
  • Withdraw consent for any further processing. Where you withdraw consent for a processing activity that is operationally essential (for example, releasing your gift), we will explain the consequences and any continuing legal retention requirement.
  • Receive notification in the event of a notifiable data breach affecting your personal data, in line with the PDPA's Data Breach Notification obligation.

Send any request to dpo@altru.asia. We will respond within 30 days. There may be a reasonable administrative fee for an access request that materially exceeds our standard workflow; we will quote this before incurring it and you may decline.

8 · How we protect your data

  • TLS 1.3 in transit; encryption at rest for all stored personal data.
  • Principle-of-least-privilege access controls. Production access requires multi-factor authentication and is logged immutably.
  • Quarterly third-party penetration testing of the platform.
  • Continuous dependency and secret scanning in our build pipeline.
  • Annual tabletop exercise of our data-breach response runbook.

9 · Data breach notification

If we suffer a personal data breach that is assessed to be notifiable under Part VIA of the PDPA, we will notify the Personal Data Protection Commission within the timeframe required by the Act and notify affected individuals where the PDPA requires us to do so, including the type of data involved, the likely impact, steps we are taking, and what you can do to mitigate harm.

10 · Cookies and similar technologies

altru.asia uses a small number of strictly necessary cookies and similar technologies (such as Workers KV-backed session tokens) required for site security and authenticated sessions. We do not use third-party advertising cookies or cross-site tracking.

11 · Children

Altru is intended for use by adults aged 18 and over. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe we have inadvertently collected such data, please contact our DPO so we can delete it.

12 · Updates to this Notice

We may update this Privacy Notice from time to time. The "Effective date" at the top of this page reflects the latest revision. Material changes will be highlighted on the site and, where appropriate, notified by email to affected users.
