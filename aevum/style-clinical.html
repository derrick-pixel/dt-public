01 / Premise

The product is not the scan.
It is what we do with the data.

/ § 01.01

A full-body MRI is, in 2026, a commodity. The same magnet is installed in tertiary hospitals across Singapore, Bangkok, Hong Kong, and Kuala Lumpur. What is rare — and what we have built AEVUM around — is the workflow that surrounds the scan.

One radiologist. One guest. Sixty minutes after the scan. Every finding mapped, contextualised, and explained — including the thirty to forty per cent of incidental findings that are statistically benign and which, in less considered settings, trigger a costly diagnostic cascade.

You leave with a report you can read, a plan you can follow, and a calibrated sense of where your body is — not where it might be.

05 / Consultation

The scan tells you what is there. The consultation tells you what to do about it.

Roughly one in three full-body scans surfaces a benign incidental finding — a small cyst, a tendon irregularity, a soft-tissue shadow. Without context, this triggers anxiety and unnecessary follow-up.

Our 60-minute consultation, conducted by the radiologist who read your scan, exists to de-escalate with evidence — to explain what is statistically normal, what merits a six-month re-look, and what (rarely) warrants a referral.

You leave with a written, hand-bound report and the name of a physician we trust at Columbia Asia, Mount Elizabeth, or Gleneagles, should one prove useful.
