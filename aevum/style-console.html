01 / Premise
i.

A scan is a moment.
Stewardship of a life is not.

A full-body MRI is, in 2026, a commodity. The same Siemens magnet sits in tertiary hospitals across Singapore, Bangkok, and Kuala Lumpur. What is rare — and what AEVUM exists to provide — is the workflow that surrounds the scan.

One radiologist. One guest. Sixty minutes after the scan, in a room with no clock. Every finding mapped, contextualised, and explained — including the ~32% of incidental findings that are statistically benign and which, in less considered settings, trigger a costly diagnostic cascade.

05 / Consultation

The scan tells you what is there. The consultation tells you what to do about it.

About 1 in 3 full-body scans surfaces a benign incidental finding — a small cyst, a tendon irregularity, a soft-tissue shadow. Without context, this triggers anxiety and unnecessary follow-up.

Our 60-minute consultation, conducted by the radiologist who read your scan, exists to de-escalate with evidence. We name our radiologists, publish a double-read protocol, and are explicit about the WB-MRI clinical-evidence debate (ACR, CAR · Sept 2025).

You leave with a written, hand-bound report and the name of a physician we trust at Columbia Asia, Mount Elizabeth, or Gleneagles, should one prove useful.
