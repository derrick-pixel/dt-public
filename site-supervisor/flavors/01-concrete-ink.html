Vol. I — Strategic Profile Elitez Group · Strategy Office Issue 01 · May 2026

On the eve of a regulator's reset, we are building the firm.

On 3 September 2025, the Building & Construction Authority announced firm-based licensing for site supervision — mandatory 2028 for projects above S$75M. We are filing for SAC accreditation in 2026 and BCA licence by Q1–Q2 2028 — under the platform of Elitez Group, with Adept Academy as the WSQ-funded training engine. The body-shop era ends.
§ I

Three forces converge between now and 2028. The macro: Singapore construction at a structural high — S$47–53B forecast for 2026, with 2027–2030 demand averaging S$39–46B per annum. The regulatory: BCA's firm-based regime closes the largest segment of the market to body shops. The platform: Elitez Group already owns most of the inputs — Adept Academy, ISO stack, ACES committee seat, Aviation/ALSS precedent — for other reasons.

Annotations Adept Academy is one of the SSG-approved Training Organisations and would be the only Tier-3 site supervision firm in Singapore with an in-house WSQ school.

ACES advisory committee seat — co-administers the Joint Accreditation Committee with IES; accredits every RE/RTO in Singapore.

Aviation/ALSS — Elitez Aviation became the exclusive supplier of certified aerospace technicians under the Aerospace Labour Supply Scheme in 2025.

§ III

A twelve to eighteen month head-start window — and a moat that does not buy.

The body-shop era ends in 2028. Pure-play supervisor agencies cannot satisfy BCA's three pillars — ISO/IEC 17020 firm systems, a permanent core of 1 RE plus 3 RTOs (or 30%) employed for twelve consecutive months, and SAC-calibrated measurement technologies — without a platform behind them.

For Elitez, the marginal cost is roughly half the greenfield equivalent (S$1.2M+) and most of it is consulting time, not capability build. Five Group assets matter: Adept Academy (the only Tier-3-segment WSQ school), the ISO 9001 / 27001 / EcoVadis Silver stack, ~2,000 deployed manpower with 6,228 historical placements, the Singapore Polytechnic and ITE Work Study partnerships, and regulatory access via the ACES advisory committee, MOM Alliance for Action, and Human Capital Partnership status.
