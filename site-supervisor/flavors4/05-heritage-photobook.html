— Chapter I · The Field —

A field of cranes, twelve to eighteen months wide.

On 3 September 2025, the Building & Construction Authority restructured site supervision around licensed firms. Mandatory 2028. Projects above S$75M. Three pillars: ISO/IEC 17020 firm-level systems, key personnel with twelve-month permanence, SAC-calibrated technical equipment.

The window between announcement and mandatory deadline measures roughly twenty-eight months. The first eight have passed. The window for filing SAC accreditation early — and holding a defensible competitive position before the body-shop incumbents are forced into consolidation — is twelve to eighteen months.

Elitez Group has been pouring this foundation since 2010. Adept Academy is an SSG-approved Training Organisation. The ISO 9001 / 27001 / EcoVadis Silver / Champion of Good stack already exists. The ACES advisory committee seat (which co-administers JAC) gives early visibility into accreditation criteria. None of this was built for site supervision. All of it converts.

— Chapter II · The Supervisors —

Quiet authority, on a noisy site.

The active C&S RE pool in Singapore is approximately 2,400. The active RTO pool is approximately 6,800. Both are listed on the IES/ACES Joint Accreditation Committee register. The deployable subset is materially smaller — many are retired, on long-term assignments, or in-house at contractors.

The structural constraint of this market is not contract availability. It is people. A senior RTO who has held JAC accreditation for ten years and has supervised three full-cycle PPVC projects is a finite resource. Pricing power follows from consistently fielding accredited supervisors when clients need them — not from contract win velocity.

Adept Academy is the only Tier-3-segment site-supervision capability in Singapore that operates an in-house WSQ-approved training school. The Built Environment WSQ track converts mid-career M&E technicians, draftsmen and foremen into RTO competence at 50–90% SSG subsidy. SCTP and CCP fund up to 90% of salary during reskill. This is the structural moat.

— Chapter IV · The Inheritance —

Aviation 2.0, with a different regulator.

In 2025, Elitez Aviation became the exclusive supplier of certified aerospace technicians under the Aerospace Labour Supply Scheme — a regulated, government-prioritised talent supply business. The playbook there transfers: regulated talent pool with formal accreditation gating supply; government scheme alignment; overseas mass-recruitment infrastructure operational across Indonesia, India, the Philippines; long-tenure deployment economics.

Site Supervision is, in effect, that playbook moved across one regulator. BCA instead of CAAS. JAC instead of B1/B2 aircraft technician accreditation. Built Environment WSQ instead of aerospace WSQ. Same operating muscle, applied to a structurally undersupplied, government-prioritised sector at the moment of regulatory inflection.

The greenfield equivalent build cost for this firm is over S$1.2M and 18 months. For Elitez Group, the marginal cost is roughly half — most of it consulting time, not capability build. The platform predates the strategy.
