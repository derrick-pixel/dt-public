Section 02

A regulatory inflection — and a twelve-to-eighteen month head-start window.

Three independent timelines converge between now and 2028. Firms that file early hold a defensible competitive position. Pure body shops lose access to the largest segment of the market.

Under the Building Control Act 1989, developers and owners appoint a Qualified Person (Supervision) — a Professional Engineer responsible for supervision of structural works under section 11. The QP(S) leads a site supervision team of Resident Engineers and Resident Technical Officers, all individually accredited by the IES/ACES Joint Accreditation Committee.1

BCA's 3 September 2025 announcement restructures this regime around licensed firms. From 2028 onward, all building projects above S$75M must engage a BCA-Licensed Site Supervision Firm. Three pillars define eligibility: (i) ISO/IEC 17020 inspection-body framework with seven-year document retention; (ii) Technical Director (PE-Civil with three-plus years' QP experience), two additional QP staff, and a permanent core of 1 RE plus 3 RTOs (or 30%) employed for at least twelve consecutive months; (iii) operational SOPs aligned to the inspection-body framework, SAC-calibrated measurement equipment, and digital storage and visualisation infrastructure.

For Elitez, the marginal cost of meeting these requirements is roughly half the greenfield equivalent (S$1.2M+ over 18 months), because the platform already exists for other reasons — Adept Academy SSG-approved Training Organisation, ISO 9001 / 27001 / EcoVadis stack, IHRP exclusive partnership, ACES advisory committee seat, and the operating playbook of Elitez Aviation under the Aerospace Labour Supply Scheme.2
