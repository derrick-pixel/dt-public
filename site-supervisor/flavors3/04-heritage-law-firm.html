— Article ii —

A letter from the Strategy Office.

The Building Control Act 1989 has, for nearly four decades, governed structural supervision in Singapore through the Qualified Person regime. Under section 11 of the Act, developers and owners appoint a Qualified Person (Supervision) — a Professional Engineer responsible for the supervision of structural works — supported by a team of Resident Engineers and Resident Technical Officers, individually accredited by the Joint Accreditation Committee (administered by the Institution of Engineers Singapore and the Association of Consulting Engineers Singapore).1

On 3 September 2025, the BCA announced that this regime will be restructured around licensed firms, mandatory from 2028 for all building projects above S$75M. Three pillars define eligibility: (i) ISO/IEC 17020 inspection-body framework with seven-year document retention; (ii) Technical Director (PE-Civil with three or more years' QP experience) supported by two additional QPs and a permanent core of one Resident Engineer plus three Resident Technical Officers (or thirty per cent of total headcount), each employed for not fewer than twelve consecutive months; (iii) operational standard operating procedures, Singapore Accreditation Council-calibrated measurement equipment, and digital storage and visualisation infrastructure.2

For Elitez Group, the marginal cost of meeting these requirements is approximately half the greenfield equivalent. The platform exists for other reasons: Adept Academy, our SkillsFuture Singapore-approved Training Organisation; ISO 9001:2015 and ISO 27001 certifications; EcoVadis Silver rating; the Institute for Human Resource Professionals exclusive partnership; the ACES advisory committee seat that co-administers the Joint Accreditation Committee; and the operating playbook of Elitez Aviation under the Aerospace Labour Supply Scheme.

The Board has approved, subject to a hard go/no-go review at Month 12 and a Y2→Y3 scaling-readiness gate at Month 18, an equity capital commitment of S$740,000 phased over fourteen months, plus authorisation for a S$1,000,000 revolving working-capital facility. We propose to file SAC accreditation in Q3 2027 and target BCA-licensed firm status by Q1–Q2 2028 — six months ahead of mandatory.
