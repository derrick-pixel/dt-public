Strategy Office

The Building Control Act 1989 has, for nearly four decades, governed structural supervision in Singapore via the Qualified Person regime. Developers and owners appoint a QP(S) — a Professional Engineer responsible for supervision under section 11 — supported by a team of Resident Engineers and Resident Technical Officers, each individually accredited by the Joint Accreditation Committee (administered by IES and ACES).

On 3 September 2025, the BCA announced that this regime will be restructured around licensed firms, mandatory from 2028 for all building projects above S$75M. The announcement is consequential. It restructures buyer screening, narrows the addressable supplier set, and creates a finite window — perhaps twelve to eighteen months — during which firms with the right platform can establish defensible competitive position ahead of competitors who must build greenfield.

For Elitez Group, the marginal cost of meeting the requirements is roughly half the greenfield equivalent (S$1.2M+ over 18 months). The platform exists for other reasons: Adept Academy SSG-approved Training Organisation; ISO 9001 and ISO 27001; EcoVadis Silver; the IHRP exclusive partnership; the ACES advisory committee seat (which co-administers JAC); and the operating playbook of Elitez Aviation under the Aerospace Labour Supply Scheme.

We propose to file SAC accreditation in Q3 2027 and target BCA-licensed firm status by Q1–Q2 2028 — six months ahead of mandatory. Under operator co-founders recruited from Surbana Jurong, on a four-year vest with one-year cliff, with funding envelope of S$700K equity and a S$1M working-capital facility. The base case projects Year-3 revenue of S$17.8M and EBITDA of S$1.6M, with equity payback in Month 28.
