Singapore's licensed site supervision firm — built before the 2028 deadline.

On 3 September 2025, the Building & Construction Authority announced firm-based licensing for site supervision — mandatory 2028 for projects above S$75M. Elitez Site Supervisor is being incorporated to file SAC accreditation in 2026 and BCA licence by Q1–Q2 2028, six months ahead of mandatory.

For nearly four decades, Singapore's structural-supervision regime has rested on the Qualified Person — a Professional Engineer responsible under section 11 of the Building Control Act 1989 — supported by a team of Resident Engineers and Resident Technical Officers, individually accredited by the Joint Accreditation Committee.

BCA's September 2025 announcement restructures the regime around licensed firms. Three pillars define eligibility: ISO/IEC 17020 inspection-body framework; key personnel including a Technical Director and a permanent core of 1 RE plus 3 RTOs employed for at least twelve consecutive months; and operational SOPs aligned to the inspection-body framework with SAC-accredited measurement equipment.

Greenfield, the cost is over S$1.2M and 18 months. For Elitez Group, the marginal cost is roughly half — because the platform exists already. Adept Academy, the SSG-approved Training Organisation. ISO 9001 / 27001 / EcoVadis Silver. The IHRP exclusive partnership. The ACES advisory committee seat that co-administers JAC. And the operating playbook of Elitez Aviation under the Aerospace Labour Supply Scheme.

The base case projects Year-3 revenue of S$17.8M and EBITDA of S$1.6M, with equity payback in Month 28. Under operator co-founders recruited from Surbana Jurong on a four-year vest with one-year cliff. Funding envelope: S$700K equity plus a S$1M working-capital facility.

Demand topology. Public agencies anchor approximately 55% of construction contract value. The civil-engineering line item — driven by Cross Island Line Phase 2/3, Changi T5, Tuas Mega Port, and NUH redevelopment — reaches a record S$11.6–13.4B in 2026, well above pre-COVID norms.

Supply-side constraint. The active C&S RE pool stands at approximately 2,400; the active RTO pool at approximately 6,800 (IES/ACES JAC register). The deployable subset is materially smaller — many are retired, on long-term assignments, or in-house at contractors. The market's binding constraint is talent supply, not contract availability.

The licence is the moat. From 2028, only BCA-licensed firms may engage on projects above S$75M. The 12-month permanence rule (30% of REs/RTOs employed for at least twelve consecutive months) explicitly disadvantages body shops with rotating headcount, and structurally favours platforms with retention infrastructure.
