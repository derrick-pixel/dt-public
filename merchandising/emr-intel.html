Platform Walkthrough

Step inside the platform.

Use the tabs below to switch between the six surfaces of the EMR-Intel platform — the three brand-facing pillars plus the operational layers underneath every visit.

Mobile App

What your merchandiser sees.

The mobile app is the front door of every visit. Your merchandiser logs in, sees their route, walks the store, captures evidence, and closes the visit — all without a back-office handoff. Multilingual support is non-trivial. Singapore-Malaysia field forces are mixed EN, CN, BM; the app ships natively in all three so training does not get lost in translation.

Capabilities
  • Attendance and GPS check-in at the outlet
  • Journey planning with route optimisation
  • Planogram compliance capture with reference photos
  • Stock check and out-of-stock logging
  • Price check with competitor capture
  • Share-of-shelf and share-of-space measurement
  • Secondary display verification
  • Promotion compliance with photo evidence
  • eContent reference — SOPs, brand books, training videos
  • Feedback workflow back to supervisor
  • Languages: English, Mandarin Chinese, Bahasa Melayu
QuickView Back-Office

What your supervisor sees.

Supervisors do not have time to navigate a BI tool between store visits. QuickView is built for the field-supervisor day — fast attendance review, exception triage, route adjustment, and audit response — on whatever device they have to hand.

Capabilities
  • Live attendance roster with exception flags
  • Route adjustment and reassignment
  • Photo review and exception sign-off
  • Audit response and corrective-action tracking
  • Supervisor-to-supervisor handoff for shift coverage
  • Escalation path to client account manager
BI Dashboards

What your brand HQ sees.

Your dashboard, not ours. The BI layer is configured per contract — KPI definitions, threshold colours, outlet groupings, and channel cuts are all tuned to how your trade-marketing team already reads the business.

Executive Summary

Single-screen read of weekly call achievement, MSL compliance, share-of-shelf, OOS, and promo compliance.

Weekly Call Achievement

Planned versus achieved visits, by route, by merchandiser, by channel.

MSL Compliance

Must-stock-list adherence across outlets, with photo-evidenced exception logging.

Share-of-Shelf / Share-of-Display

Linear shelf measurement against competitor SKUs, with end-cap and secondary-display roll-up.

Promo Compliance

Promotion run-out verification — pricing, signage, end-cap, planogram.

Daily OOS Trend

Out-of-stock by SKU, by outlet, by day. Trend lines flag persistent versus transient gaps.

Competitor Price-Check

Price tracking on adjacent SKUs, captured during the visit.

Photo Gallery

Every visit produces evidence. Searchable by outlet, date, SKU, and exception type.

Attendance & Activity

Field-force time on store, time at outlet, and route compliance.

Photo Evidence

Every claim, photographically defended.

A KPI without a photo is an argument. Every shelf observation, planogram check, OOS log, and promo verification is captured with a timestamped, geotagged image and pushed to your dashboard before the merchandiser leaves the aisle. When a brand manager asks why a number moved, the answer is a gallery — not a hand-wave.

Capabilities
  • Timestamped, geotagged photo capture on every observation
  • Reference-photo overlay for planogram and POSM verification
  • Exception photo trail linked to corrective-action workflow
  • Searchable gallery by outlet, date, SKU, and exception type
  • Retention policy configured per contract — PDPA-aligned
  • Audit pull: export full evidence packs per outlet or per campaign
Multilingual

Three languages on the shelf.

The Singapore-Malaysia field force is mixed-language. A merchandiser walking a pharmacy in Bukit Timah, a supermarket in Tampines, and a beauty counter in Orchard may not share a first language with each other or with the brand team in HQ. EMR-Intel ships natively in English, Mandarin Chinese, and Bahasa Melayu — every screen, every SOP, every training video. Training translates better when the platform speaks the same language as the person walking the aisle.

Capabilities
  • Full UI translation across EN, CN, BM — no half-translated screens
  • Per-user language preference, switchable at any time
  • Multilingual SOP and brand-book references inside the app
  • Training videos with subtitles in all three languages
  • Supervisor escalations translated for the receiving party
  • Field-tested with Singapore-Malaysia mixed-language teams since 2011
Ops Room

The room behind the field force.

EMR-Intel is not just software — it is software backed by a staffed operations room at Elitez HQ. Live attendance, live exceptions, live escalations, watched by trained operators during trading hours. When a merchandiser does not show up, when a route is at risk, when a brand-critical outlet flags an exception — the response starts in the ops room before it reaches your inbox.

Capabilities
  • Live attendance and exception monitoring during trading hours
  • First-response triage — replacement deployment, route reassignment
  • Escalation matrix from operator → supervisor → account manager
  • Cross-contract bench coordination for emergency cover
  • Daily morning-brief and end-of-day exception report
  • Direct line to your trade-marketing lead for brand-critical events
