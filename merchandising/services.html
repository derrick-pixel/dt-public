Services

Five service lines. One operational bench.

Dedicated teams, shared cover, trade-marketing execution, audit, and the EMR-Intel platform — scoped to your roster and your KPIs. Engagement is structured around your in-store execution metrics, not a generic service catalogue.

Dedicated Merchandising 01

A full-time team scoped to one brand.

Your bench, badged, trained, and supervised. Team sizes range from 5 to 60 pax depending on coverage. We handle recruitment, training, routing, supervision, KPI reporting, and the replacement of any under-performer at no additional cost. Recommended when in-store execution is a sustained, year-round KPI — e.g., share-of-shelf defence, planogram compliance, promotional run-outs, NPI coverage, and competitor monitoring across hundreds of outlets per week.

What is in scope
  • Recruitment, onboarding, and structured training
  • Daily routing and outlet coverage planning
  • Supervisor layer with field audits and exception handling
  • KPI reporting against MSL, share-of-shelf, OOS, planogram, promo compliance
  • Free replacement of any merchandiser on under-performance
  • EMR-Intel bundled — mobile app, QuickView, BI dashboards

Best fit

Brands with sustained in-store presence and multi-million-dollar trade investment to protect.

Shared Merchandising 02

Auxiliary cover, without a full-time roster.

Up-to-six-pax shared bench providing ad-hoc coverage across supermarkets, pharmacies, and convenience. Available on short notice. Cost-efficient for brands whose coverage needs are seasonal, geographically narrow, or layered on top of an existing team. Shared bench operates on the same EMR-Intel platform as the dedicated model — your dashboard does not change when you scale up or down.

What is in scope
  • Ad-hoc and recurring cover across selected outlets
  • Same supervision standard as the dedicated bench
  • EMR-Intel reporting across the assigned routes
  • Turn-key onboarding measured in days, not weeks
  • Scale up to a dedicated team without re-tendering

Best fit

Challenger brands, seasonal SKUs, NPI launches, and gap-filling around an existing in-house team.

Trade-Marketing Execution 03

Promotions land in the aisle, not just in the brief.

POSM deployment, space-buy execution, secondary display build-out, and NPI launch coverage. Trade-marketing execution sits between your campaign team and the retailer — we close the gap that is usually filled by goodwill and weekend favours. Retailers move quickly; your trade calendar does not always sync with theirs. We hold the field-level relationships and the staging capacity to convert a brief into a built display by the agreed launch date.

What is in scope
  • POSM staging, transport, and in-aisle installation
  • Secondary display and end-cap build-out
  • Space-buy execution across major retail chains
  • NPI launch coverage with photo evidence and compliance reporting
  • Field training for promoter-driven activations

Best fit

Brand teams running NPI launches, seasonal campaigns, or end-cap takeovers in tier-1 retail.

Merchandising Audit 04

Third-party verification of what is actually in-store.

When you need an independent read of in-store execution — yours or someone else's — we field a structured audit programme with clear channel coverage, lead-time reporting, and workflow tracking modelled on the 2024/25 L'Oréal merchandising and audit programme. Audits are run by trained auditors, not the same teams executing day-to-day merchandising, so the read is independent and defensible.

What is in scope
  • Structured outlet sampling across supermarkets and pharmacies
  • Planogram, share-of-shelf, OOS, pricing, and promo compliance checks
  • Workflow tracking with clear lead-time reporting
  • Photo evidence and exception logs in EMR-Intel
  • Independent auditor pool — segregated from execution teams

Best fit

Brand HQ teams verifying field execution, or retailers auditing supplier compliance.

EMR-Intel Platform 05

The operating system underneath every contract.

EMR-Intel is not sold separately. It ships bundled with every dedicated, shared, trade-marketing, and audit engagement — the mobile app your merchandiser carries, the QuickView back-office your supervisor lives in, and the BI dashboards your brand HQ reads on Monday mornings. The platform is the reason the other four service lines are defensible at scale: same data, three surfaces, multilingual, evidenced on every visit.

What is in scope
  • Mobile app for the field — EN, CN, BM
  • QuickView back-office for field supervisors
  • BI dashboards configured to your KPI definitions
  • Photo-evidence capture on every observation
  • Ops-room monitoring during trading hours
  • Power BI / Tableau / CSV / SFTP export — your trade-marketing stack stays intact

Best fit

Bundled inside every Elitez Merchandising contract — there is no standalone licence.

Engagement Model

How a contract starts.

A predictable on-boarding sequence. Each step is owned by a named person on our side — no diffuse accountability.

Step 01

Scoping call

Fifteen minutes with Jack Wang. We confirm category, channels, coverage scope, and KPI priorities.

Step 02

Proposal

Written scope within one week — headcount, routing, KPI definitions, EMR-Intel configuration, and commercials.

Step 03

Recruitment & training

We draw from the existing bench wherever possible. Net-new headcount goes through structured recruitment and category-specific training.

Step 04

Deployment & shake-out

First four weeks are a deliberate calibration window — routes adjusted, KPIs locked, supervisor escalation paths verified.

Step 05

Steady-state

Weekly KPI review, monthly business review, quarterly strategy review. EMR-Intel dashboards are live to your team throughout.

Service Standard

Under-performance is our cost, not yours.

If a merchandiser is not pulling weight against agreed KPIs, we replace them at no extra cost. The penalty for under-performance sits with the agency, not with the brand. It is the simplest version of an accountability promise we have been able to write.
