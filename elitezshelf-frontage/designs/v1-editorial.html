Editorial

For two decades the trade marketing manager has been asked to defend a category position with data that arrives a month late, in a panel summary, from a vendor whose own field collectors visit a fraction of the relevant outlets. Trax answers the cadence question with fixed cameras. NielsenIQ answers the breadth question with monthly panels. Both answers cost six figures, and neither covers the heartland NTUC outlet where 60% of your SOS volume actually moves.

ElitezShelf reframes the question. The expensive part was never the camera — it was the routed merchandiser visit. We already make 370 such visits every week, across 230 NTUC FairPrice outlets and 74 Sheng Siong stores. Equip those visits with a S$280 body-cam, decode the footage with computer vision tuned to local SKU formats, and the marginal cost of weekly intelligence collapses to four minutes of upload time.

The structural argument lands hardest in three buyer archetypes: mid-market FMCG owners priced out of Trax's six-figure floor, niche import distributors shipping into Don Don Donki and Mustafa where global SOS tools have no panel, and DTC challengers who need a 4-week launch evidence pack to walk into the week-5 retailer review with photos rather than rep texts.

For each, the pitch is the same: pick three SKUs, pick three retailers, get four weeks of weekly evidence — at half the headline of the incumbent and PSG-eligible at the Trade tier. Either it changes how you negotiate the next planogram, or you don't pay.
