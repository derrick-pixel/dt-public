    Section 03

    Executive Summary

    Five anchor findings. Each is backed by the data appendix that follows in Sections 4–9.

    1. The SG marketing retainer mid-market is structurally underserved. Premium PR agencies (Mutant, Naga DDB Tribal) reject sub-S$5K retainers. Mid-tier digital agencies (2Stallions, Verz, iFoundries, Construct Digital) lock prospects into 6-month commitments and gate pricing behind enquiry forms. Freelancer marketplaces (Fiverr, Upwork, Freelance.sg) cannot ship a full lead-gen system. Nobody packages a retainer-native, 30-day-notice offer with monthly CPL reporting at the S$799–S$2,999 SME tier.
    2. Why Pulse wins — ops-stack arbitrage, not geographic arbitrage. Published Entry / Core / Premium pricing (S$799 / S$1,499 / S$2,999). 30-day notice vs industry-standard 6-month lock. Monthly one-page CPL / ROAS report. AI layer collapses delivery hours; Elitez Group's 15yr lineage + 350-staff backbone + 90+ ongoing client operations absorb the rent and senior-AM tax that micro-agencies load onto retainers. Margin = SG price − AI-reduced hours − group-shared overhead.
    3. Top three white-space niches. (1) Bootstrapped B2B SME — "Proof, not Performance Theatre" targeting ~3,200 SG bootstrapped 10–40-employee firms. (2) Pro-Services SME — "Trust Agency, Not Vanity Agency" targeting ~5,800 SG law / accounting / engineering firms. (3) Gen-2 Family Business — "The Modernisation Retainer" targeting ~2,400 SG family SMEs in leadership transition.
    4. Design audit reality. Industry average 6.7 across twelve SG / SEA / global subjects on a 5-dimension rubric; Pulse scores 6.8, mid-pack — ahead of MediaOne (5.2), GoDigital (5.0) and Verz (6.2); behind Naga DDB Tribal (8.4), Mutant (7.8) and Fiverr Pro (7.8). Gap to leader is 1.6 — almost entirely on credibility artefacts (named case studies, audited retention) and visual brand depth. Brand distinctiveness already beats SG SME peers.
    5. Five recommended actions. Surface the Elitez 15yr / 350-staff / 90+ operations trust strip on every public page. Ship a downloadable Q1 ROI Report PDF with anonymised CPL data. Build vertical-led landing pages for the four ICPs (B2B / pro-services / family-business / regulated). Add a sticky '2-min ROI Diagnostic' CTA on every template. Publish three named case studies with CPL + retention markers.

    Section 09

    Recommendations

    Five action items. Each one connects a real Pulse advantage to the place a buyer is most likely to decide.

    1. Surface the Elitez group trust strip on every page
      Move "Elitez Group · 15 years · 350+ staff · 90+ ongoing client operations" into a persistent strip at the top of every public page. Today this lives only in /about; the buyer is not reading it where they decide.
    2. Ship a downloadable Q1 ROI Report PDF
      Anonymised CPL / ROAS / retention figures from a representative Core-tier client. Quote the binding 30-day-notice clause inside. Convert the AI-augmented delivery, group-shared overhead, and ops-stack arbitrage story into a single defensible artefact a procurement officer can email around.
    3. Vertical-led landing pages for four ICPs
      Four distinct entry points: B2B Services, Pro-Services SME (law / accounting / engineering), Gen-2 Family Business, Regulated (education / healthcare / training). Each with its own hero copy, its own CTA, and its own proof stack — the bootstrapped B2B page leads with the S$999 entry tier and 90-day outcome promise; the pro-services page leads with MAS / Law Society compliance awareness.
    4. Sticky 2-min ROI Diagnostic CTA on every template
      Replace the static contact button with a sticky "Run my 2-min ROI Diagnostic" CTA. The diagnostic captures industry, headcount, current spend, deal size, urgency — and outputs a tier recommendation plus break-even maths live. Quote the SGD 42 cost-per-lead benchmark, 47 leads/month median, 6–9 week payback as the anchor.
    5. Publish three named case studies with CPL + retention markers
      Pulse already has the math; what's missing is the receipt. Three named-client case studies with monthly CPL data, total leads, retention through month 12, and an honest mention of the 30-day-notice trigger if any. Closes the credibility gap that puts Pulse behind Mutant on the design audit.
