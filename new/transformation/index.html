The track record · Elitez Group

Transformation isn't a theory I sell. It's something I've led three times.

People talk about organisational transformation. As Group CEO of Elitez — a manpower and HR group across 9 Asian cities with a 1,500-strong workforce — I've had to steer the company through three of them, back to back. Through a crisis. Through a reckoning. And now through a reinvention.

2020–22 Crisis & COVID 2021–24 ESG transformation 2024–now AI transformation
01
2020 – 2022
Crisis leadership

Steering a people business through a pandemic.

When COVID hit, it hit Elitez where it lives. A manpower and recruitment group survives on people moving, working and being deployed — and overnight, all three stopped.

Hiring froze. Borders closed. In Singapore, the dormitory outbreaks struck the very contract workforce our business is built around. For a company whose product is people, this was existential, not inconvenient.

"My job that year wasn't growth. It was to protect the workforce, keep the company solvent, and come out the other side fitter than we went in."

So I led from the front: stabilising cash and operations, protecting jobs and worker welfare, and digitalising processes that had run on paper and proximity for years. What had been a crisis response became a permanent upgrade — a leaner, more digital, more resilient Elitez.

Protected the workforce
Prioritised jobs, welfare and continuity for staff and contract workers through the worst of it.
Digitalised operations
Moved manual, in-person recruitment and ops workflows online — and never moved them back.
Held the group solvent
Defended cash and core services so the business emerged intact, not just alive.
Built crisis muscle
Turned emergency adaptation into an operating habit the group still runs on.
02
2021 – 2024
Responsible growth

Rebuilding Elitez as a company worth trusting.

As the world recovered, the bar moved. Clients, regulators and talent stopped asking only what we delivered and started asking how. As Head of ESG, I made answering that question a transformation, not a checkbox.

For a manpower group, ESG isn't an environmental afterthought — the "S" is the whole business. Fair, ethical and transparent employment. Migrant- and contract-worker welfare. Workforce development and upskilling. I set out to make responsible employment a structural advantage, backed by real governance rather than a glossy report.

"In a people business, ESG done seriously isn't compliance overhead. It's the trust your whole brand is built on."

I earned my own ESG credentials to lead it credibly, embedded governance and reporting discipline across the group, and positioned Elitez as a responsible employer ahead of the curve rather than scrambling to catch it. By 2024, ESG had moved from a side initiative to part of how Elitez wins and retains business.

Owned the agenda
Took the Head of ESG mandate and earned ESG certification to lead it from the front.
Put the "S" first
Fair employment, worker welfare and workforce development as core, not cosmetic.
Embedded governance
Reporting and governance discipline built into how the group actually operates.
Made it commercial
Turned responsible employment into client trust and a reason to choose Elitez.
03
2024 – now
Reinvention

And now, the AI transformation.

Same playbook, new frontier. Having led Elitez through crisis and through ESG, I'm now rebuilding how the work itself gets done — with AI, by my own hand.

In under three months I stood up an AI department and shipped 20+ working systems across sales, retail operations, learning and HR. It's the third transformation in five years — and the one I now help other HR and SME leaders take on themselves.
