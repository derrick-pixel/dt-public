    Section 03

    Executive Summary

    Five anchor findings. Each is grounded in the data files in /data/ and re-derived in Sections 4–10.

    1. The SG payroll market is structurally bifurcated. Global EOR (Deel, Remote, Multiplier, Papaya, G-P) charges US$400-1,000+/employee/month — designed for cross-border / mid-market hiring, ~13× too expensive for an SG-local 1-3 employee micro-business. SG payroll software (Talenox, HReasily, Justlogin, Payboy) charges S$5-15/emp/mo but is DIY — the owner still does the work and carries the EOR liability.
    2. Why Elitez wins. Flat S$59/employee/month with full EOR liability transfer. CardUp credit-card-funded payroll → 1.75-2× SG miles per S$ paid — convert your existing payroll spend into a yearly family trip. SG-only depth: CPF, SDL, WICA, IR8A, foreign worker levies all native, not bolted on. Self-serve onboarding designed for 1-3 employee businesses — sign up and run first payroll in 60 minutes.
    3. Top three white-space niches. (1) Hawker / F&B micro-tier — 12,000+ SG hawker stalls + boutique cafes typically using sole-proprietor payroll, manual or outdated. (2) TCM / allied healthcare clinic — 2-5 employee clinics with CPF + WICA needs and zero EOR coverage. (3) Boutique retail e-commerce — 2-5 part-timer shops where founder-spend on inventory + payroll could fund family trips via miles.
    4. Design audit reality. Industry average 7.2 across seven players on a 5-dimension rubric; Elitez scores 7.4, third behind Deel (8.6) and Multiplier (8.0). Gap to leader is 1.2 points — almost entirely on visual brand distinctiveness depth and audited enterprise proof. The fix is to surface what already exists: CardUp-miles calculator on hero, named SG SME case studies, audited customer count.
    5. Five recommended actions. Surface CardUp-miles savings calculator above the fold. Ship 3 named SG micro-business case studies (hawker, cafe, TCM clinic). Publish audited "Why $59 works" technical brief. Build hawker / F&B vertical landing page. Live miles-credited counter on hero pulling from public ledger.

        Section 09

        Recommendations

        Five deck-grounded action items. Each one connects an Elitez proof point already in the public site or admin data files to the place a buyer is most likely to decide.

        1. Surface the CardUp-miles savings calculator on the homepage hero
          Move the "S$X payroll → Y miles → return-flight value" calculator into the hero zone of index.html and into a persistent strip at the top of every pricing page. Today this lives 1-2 clicks deep; the buyer is not seeing it where they decide. Default the calculator to a 2-employee S$3,000 hawker payroll so the headline number ("≈ 6,000 miles/yr — 1 SG-KL family flight") lands in the first 5 seconds.
        2. Ship 3 named SG SME case studies (hawker / cafe / TCM clinic)
          Add a "Real SG Micro-Businesses Using Elitez" section with three named operators: a hawker stall (sole-prop with 1 helper), a boutique cafe (founder + 2 baristas), a TCM clinic (founder + 1 receptionist). Each card with verifiable metrics — hours saved per month, miles earned, CPF / WICA filings handled. This closes the audited-proof gap that puts Elitez behind Deel / Multiplier on the design audit.
        3. Persona-led landing pages for the top hawker / cafe / TCM / retail verticals
          Four distinct entry points: Hawker / F&B micro, Boutique cafe, TCM / allied healthcare clinic, Boutique retail / e-comm. Each with its own hero copy, its own CTA, its own proof stack — the hawker page leads with sole-prop CPF + casual labour handling; the cafe page leads with part-timer + tipping handling; the TCM page leads with WICA + practitioner licensing. Incumbents homogenise the funnel; Elitez differentiates by vertical.
        4. Live miles-credited counter widget on the hero
          A counter that reads "X million SG miles credited to micro-businesses YTD — and counting" on the homepage hero, updating live as customer payroll runs. Pulls from the same data source the audited charity ledger uses, so the counter and the public report cannot drift out of sync. This is the single most defensible visual artefact Elitez can ship — no SG payroll competitor has a comparable signal.
        5. Audited "Why $59 works for 1-3 employee businesses" technical brief
          PDF download with cost-stack breakdown: salary + 17% CPF + 0.25% SDL + WICA premium + Elitez S$59 fee + miles offset = a defensible margin model that explains why the price cannot be undercut by SG payroll software DIY. Co-author with a named SG SME accountant for credibility. Distribute to PSG-eligibility consultants, AIC SME outreach, hawker-association partners.
