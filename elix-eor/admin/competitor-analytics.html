          SG payroll compliance stack — CPF, SDL, WICA, IR8A

          The Singapore micro-business cost stack is statutorily defined. Employer CPF for under-55 is 17% of ordinary wages capped at OW S$8,000/mo (2026 ceiling, up from S$7,400 in 2025). SDL is 0.25% capped at S$11.25/mo per employee. WICA insurance is mandatory. IR8A is filed annually via IRAS AIS. These are the numbers every EOR / payroll provider in this dataset must price around.

          Themes from the data

          1. Global EOR pricing band (US$400-1000+/emp/mo) — designed for cross-border / mid-market hiring. ~13× more expensive than Elitez's flat S$59 for an SG-local 1-3 employee micro-business. The micro-tier was structurally underserved.
          2. SG payroll software band (S$5-15/emp/mo) — Talenox, HReasily, Justlogin, Payboy. DIY: the business owner still does the work. Not full EOR; no employer-of-record liability transfer.
          3. SG payroll outsourcing band (S$5-20/payslip + S$50-100/mo base) — accounting firms and dedicated payroll bureaus. Hands-off but no group benefits, no CardUp miles, no employer-of-record liability shift.
          4. PSG grant offset (50%) — Payboy and a handful of pre-approved IMDA vendors qualify for 50% Productivity Solutions Grant. Reduces SG payroll software effective price to S$2.50-5/emp/mo. Elitez's flat S$59 is harder to beat on absolute price for tiny companies but wins on liability transfer + miles.
          5. Nobody offers airline-miles via CardUp on payroll — the entire competitive set ignores the rewards-on-business-spend lever. CardUp credit-card-funded payroll → 1.75-2x SG miles per S$ paid → hawker / cafe / TCM owners can fund a yearly family trip from existing payroll spend. This is Elitez's most defensible wedge.
