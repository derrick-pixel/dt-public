§ III — Where this offering fits

The bundle does what none of the nine do.

Bespoke + strategic + fast + auditable + mid-market-priced. This is the gap none of the nine archetypes fill in one buy.

vs MBB / Tier-2
10–80× cheaper, days not months
MBB and Tier-2 sell 8–16-week diagnostics with a 5-person team. This is 5–10 days with one operator + 9 sub-agents. Less depth on primary-source interviews; not a fit for "betting the company" decisions.
Sells against an MBB diagnostic the buyer can't afford or doesn't need at that depth.
vs CI software (Klue / Crayon)
Same dollar tier, real strategic outputs
Klue and Crayon at USD 20–80k/yr give live alerts and battlecards but no sizing, no whitespace atlas, no pricing tiers. This offering at the same dollar tier delivers the strategy artefacts CI software is missing.
Sells alongside CI software, not against — different jobs.
vs syndicated research (IDC / Gartner)
Bespoke to your competitor list
IDC / Gartner / Forrester sell category-level views. They don't score the 30 firms you care about against the 7 dimensions your buyer evaluates on. Their custom research starts at USD 50k and takes 8–12 weeks.
Sells against the limitations of off-the-shelf reports.
vs off-the-shelf (Frost / Euromonitor)
A category report is the input, not the answer
A USD 5–15k Frost & Sullivan report sizes the category. It does not tell you which competitors block your wedge, which segments to attack first, or what tiers should cost. This offering uses such reports as inputs and produces the answer.
Sells as the layer above syndicated research.
vs SG independent consultant
Throughput a single human cannot match
A single SGD 3k/day SG strategy consultant takes 6–8 weeks for comparable depth across 30 competitors + market intel + pricing + whitespace. Same total budget buys ~25–30 days of one operator. This delivers the same depth in 1–2 weeks because the agents do the parallel work.
Sells as the productivity arbitrage of an experienced consultant + AI agents.

§ VI — Pre-empted objections

The pushback buyers will raise.

"Why does this cost more than a Frost & Sullivan report?"

Frost & Sullivan is shelfware: the same report sells to your competitor. This is bespoke to your 30-firm competitive set, your price points, your personas, and your wedge. Different deliverable; different decision-grade.

"Why does this cost less than McKinsey?"

McKinsey runs 30+ expert interviews, builds primary-source models, and stages 6 partner workshops. This runs 9 named sub-agents over a structured rubric, produces a written record of every assumption, and ships in 5–10 days.

"Will the structure compromise insight quality?"

Opposite. Every claim in the deliverable is auditable through the FIELD-DICTIONARY contract: every competitor field, every TAM stack, every implication has a documented derivation. MBB decks rarely show their working. The structure forces honesty.

"Can we just buy the template and run it ourselves?"

Yes — the template is open-source MIT. The price reflects the operator's judgement: which 30 competitors are the right 30, where the heatmap dimensions should sit, which attack plan is realistic vs aspirational. The agents do the labour; the operator does the editorial.

"What if my market is non-Singapore?"

The methodology generalises (the agents are not SG-specific). Pricing should land 0–25% below SG levels for SEA, ~equal for AU/HK, and 30–60% above for NA / EU markets where buyer ACVs and consultancy benchmarks are higher.

"What if the report is wrong?"

Every fact is sourced (FIELD-DICTIONARY §5.1.2 implications must cite, §5.5 every multiplier must have a source or be flagged "estimated; to be validated"). Errors are correctable in hours because the underlying JSON is the source of truth — there is no PowerPoint deck to re-cut.

§ VII — Confidence note & sources

What's high-confidence, what isn't.

Reliability tiers: MBB / Tier-2 / Big-4 / syndicated research / off-the-shelf-report ranges are high confidence — well-documented globally, cross-referenced across Vault, Glassdoor, public client testimonials, and the firms' own RFP responses.
