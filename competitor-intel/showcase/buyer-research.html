§ II — The Singapore buyer

Cost-conscious. Cash-flow-anxious. Grant-aware.

The SG SME founder / CMO / Head of Strategy sits in a specific psychological territory: they know they need competitive intelligence and market sizing — they're being asked for it by their board, their investors, and their bank — but they've been burned by either (a) Big-Four bloated decks at SGD 80k+ that didn't lead anywhere, or (b) cheap freelance work that didn't hold up under scrutiny.

Demographics
99% of SG enterprises are SMEs
369,500 SMEs. Mostly services-sector. Average labour cost share is 4–36% of total business costs depending on industry. Founder-led at the smaller end; professional management at the upper end (USD 20–100M revenue).
The funnel is wide; the question is which sub-segment actually pays for strategy work.
Marketing & research budget
5–10% of revenue (established) · 12–20% (growth-stage)
Per Marketing Benchmarks Singapore 2026: established profitable companies spend 5–10% of revenue on marketing (60% digital, 40% offline). Growth-stage SMEs go to 12–20%. Early-stage uses fixed monthly budgets of SGD 2–5k. Market research / competitor intelligence is not broken out as a line item — it's bundled inside the broader marketing envelope.
A USD 30M-revenue SG company has SGD 2.0–4.0M annual marketing budget. USD 4k for the Standard pack is 0.1–0.2% of that — well within "trial spend."
Digitalisation pain
56% say digitalisation is "too expensive"
Cited in industry research on SG SME digitalisation barriers. The pain is most acute at the SGD 5,000–30,000 spend band — where a project requires a department-head sign-off but isn't large enough to justify a formal RFP and vendor-bake-off.
Sub-SGD-5k pricing dramatically reduces the friction. This is exactly the USD 1,500 / USD 4,000 Lite + Standard band.
Consultant fee perception
SGD 150–1,500/hr range; clients pay 20% more for proven track record
Singapore Business Review study: clients willing to pay up to 20% premium for consultants with proven industry-specific track record. Niche consultancies bill SGD 150–400/hr; large international firms (Deloitte, BCG) SGD 800–1,500/hr+. Most SMEs prefer fixed project pricing over hourly because outcomes are easier to scope.
Productized fixed-price tiers (Lite USD 1,500 / Standard USD 4,000 / Pro USD 7,000) match the SME's preferred procurement shape. Hourly pricing puts off this buyer.
Grant access
PSG 70% to SGD 30k cap · EDG 70% on strategy projects
From 1 April 2026, PSG and EDG are at 70% subsidy (up from 50%). EDG explicitly covers "market research, market entry strategies, business development" — exactly the Elitez Vantage scope. EDGE framework consolidates PSG + EDG + MRA in 2H 2026.
If Elitez Vantage becomes EDG-eligible, USD 4,000 Standard becomes USD 1,200 effective — fundamentally re-prices the offering.
§ III — The Hong Kong buyer

Cash-flow first. Growth optimist. Government-funded.

DBS Hong Kong SME Survey (January 2026, n=368 SME owners and decision-makers) is the strongest dataset on this segment. The picture: a buyer who wants to grow, knows they need to internationalise, expects government funding to be part of the path, and rates "cash flow" above every other concern.

2026 outlook
60% project revenue growth · 87% maintaining or increasing budget
Of the 368 SME respondents, 50% see neutral economic outlook, 34% optimistic. Nearly 60% project revenue growth in 2026; 31% expect ≥10% growth. 87% plan to maintain or increase investment budget.
Budget is available; the question is whether competitor intelligence wins the line item.
Top concerns
53% cash flow · 53% economic fluctuations · 48% operational costs
Cash flow management ties for #1 with economic fluctuations. Anything that doesn't directly improve cash-flow visibility or reduce cost is harder to sell to a HK SME owner. The pitch must lead with "this clarifies your pricing decisions" not "this is interesting strategic insight."
Lead with the pricing-tier output, not the competitor-database output. The pricing-tier work is the cash-flow-relevant artefact.
Expansion intent
68% planning new-market expansion within 2–3 years
41% targeting Greater Bay Area cities; 29% other Asian markets; 28% Europe. Market entry is the active wedge — a HK SME owner approaching GBA expansion needs exactly the TAM/SAM/SOM funnel + competitor map that Elitez Vantage produces.
Position the offering as "what to do before you commit to a new market" — specifically the pre-expansion diagnostic.
Government dependency
50% want government funding · 42% market expansion assistance
Half the surveyed HK SMEs cite government funding as their top desired support. TVP (75% subsidy up to HKD 600,000), BUD Fund (up to HKD 7M), DTSPP (HKD 50,000) all feed into this expectation. The buyer EXPECTS subsidy as part of the package.
For HK, structure a "TVP-friendly" project scope: HKD 30–55k pre-subsidy → HKD 7.5–14k effective post-75% subsidy.
Digital maturity gap
90% integrating AI but only 26% have dedicated cyber staff
From Hong Kong SME Outlook 2026: 90% of HK businesses are integrating AI, but only 26% of SMEs have dedicated cybersecurity staff vs 59% in large enterprises. 14% of SMEs have not adopted any AI/digital tech. The "AI-driven competitor intel" pitch must address the trust gap directly.
Lean into the methodology-appendix + auditable-sources angle. The HK buyer's AI hesitation is real but addressable with proof of working.

§ VI — The fair-value gap

The buyer needs it. Doesn't know what to pay. Tells the seller.

Your anecdotal observation is the single most important finding in this document — and it's structurally backed by the data. SG/HK SME owners need competitive intelligence (the DBS HK survey shows 53% prioritising cost management; 68% planning expansion; both literally require this work) but the category has no published "fair value" reference because it's never been productized for this segment. The buyer is anchored on whatever they last heard.

The buyer compares against the wrong reference

If their last quote was a Big-Four diagnostic at USD 100k, they think "USD 4k? Too good to be true." If their last quote was a USD 49 Statista report, they think "USD 4k? Why so much for similar content?" Both reactions show the gap — neither is the right reference frame. The seller has to set the frame in the conversation.

Fixed pricing settles the anchoring problem

The "From Fees to Value" guide on SG consulting confirms: "Most SMEs and startups today prefer fixed project pricing — makes costs predictable and helps focus on outcomes." Hourly billing forces the buyer to do mental math under uncertainty; fixed tiers tell them what to budget. The three-tier ladder (USD 1.5k / 4k / 7k) is the fair-value disclosure.

56% of SG SMEs say digitalisation is "too expensive"

This isn't a price problem; it's an uncertainty problem. Buyers price in worst-case fear when they can't bound the cost. The Lite tier at USD 1,500 — fits on a corporate card, no procurement, ships in 3–5 days — is a calibration product. The buyer pays a small amount once, sees what they get, and now knows what Standard or Pro is worth.

The 100% rework guarantee is the fair-value contract

The standard sale-cycle objection is "what if it's not what I expected?" The rework guarantee removes that risk asymmetrically — buyer pays USD 4k, gets a full rework if the readout call doesn't meet bar, full refund if rework still doesn't. This converts price anxiety into a try-before-final-judgement transaction.

Grant subsidy resets the anchor decisively

If Elitez Vantage achieves EDG pre-approval (it covers "market research, market entry strategies, business development" at 70% support), the Standard tier becomes USD 1,200 effective. At that price, the fair-value question evaporates — it's "yes, obvious yes" for any SG SME planning expansion or repositioning. Same applies to TVP (75%, up to HKD 600k) for HK.

The recurring artefact resolves the value question over time

One-shot work always faces the "is this worth USD 4k?" doubt. Quarterly Refresh subscription (USD 990/qtr) reframes the question as "is the cost of NOT having this lower than USD 990 every 3 months?" — and for any business with a moving competitor set, the answer is no. Recurring revenue is also where sustainable differentiation lives.

§ VII — Grant subsidy landscape

The single biggest price-perception lever.

For SG and HK SMEs, the effective price of consultancy is often the post-subsidy price, not the headline. Becoming a pre-approved vendor on these schemes is the highest-leverage marketing investment for Elitez Vantage Phase 1.

SG · PSG
70% subsidy · capped SGD 30k/yr
Productivity Solutions Grant — pre-approved consultancy and digital solutions. From 1 Apr 2026: 70% support (was 50%). Cap SGD 30k/company/financial year. Application via GoBusiness portal. Pre-approval requires a vendor application + IMDA / Enterprise SG assessment.
Standard tier USD 4k (SGD 5.4k) post-subsidy = SGD 1.6k effective. Decisive.
SG · EDG
70% on strategy / market research projects
Enterprise Development Grant — covers "market research, market entry strategies, business development, setting up overseas operations." Direct semantic match for Elitez Vantage scope. From 1 Apr 2026: 70% support. EDGE framework consolidates EDG + PSG + MRA in 2H 2026.
Pro tier USD 7k (SGD 9.5k) post-subsidy = SGD 2.8k. Best fit for buyers planning overseas expansion (most active EDG use case).
SG · MRA
Market Readiness Assistance · overseas market entry
Specifically for SG companies expanding overseas. Covers market entry research, in-market business development, branding/IP. Strong fit for the HK + GBA expansion buyer Elitez Vantage targets.
Position Pro tier as "MRA-eligible diagnostic" for SG companies entering HK / GBA / SEA.
HK · TVP
75% subsidy · up to HKD 600,000
Technology Voucher Programme. Highest subsidy ratio of any HK scheme. Covers IT solutions, cloud services, advisory work that improves productivity. Pre-approval via HKPC.
Pro tier HKD 54k pre-subsidy = HKD 13.5k effective. Standard tier HKD 31k = HKD 7.7k effective. Decisive in HK.
HK · BUD Fund
Up to HKD 7M · brand + Mainland / FTA expansion
Branding, Upgrading and Domestic Sales fund. Targets HK SMEs expanding into Mainland China and FTA markets. Higher ceiling but project-scoped (not productized service-friendly).
Less direct fit but worth noting for the GBA-expansion buyer who's already running BUD-funded work.
§ VIII — Implications for Elitez Vantage GTM

Six moves this research changes.

Each is concrete, falsifiable, and has a 60–90 day window.

01

Apply for PSG & EDG pre-approval immediately

EDG covers "market research, market entry strategies" verbatim — Elitez Vantage scope. 70% subsidy resets the buyer's price anchor decisively. Effective USD 4k → SGD 1.6k. This is the single highest-leverage move available.

02

Apply for HKPC TVP pre-approval

75% subsidy in HK. Pro tier HKD 54k → HKD 13.5k effective. The DBS HK survey shows 50% of HK SMEs explicitly want government funding support; matching that expectation removes a primary objection.

03

Lead with the cash-flow / pricing-tier output, not the competitor database

HK SMEs prioritise cash flow + cost management (53%). The pricing-tier recommendation is the cash-flow-relevant artefact — it answers "what should I charge?" The competitor database answers "who are the threats?" Order matters in the pitch.

04

Position Lite (USD 1,500) as the calibration product

The "fair value" question dissolves once the buyer has bought once. Lite tier is corporate-card-friendly in both SG and HK, ships in 3–5 days, and gives the buyer the data point they need to evaluate Standard/Pro. Treat it as the funnel top, not as a profit centre.

05

Frame Pro tier around overseas-expansion use case

68% of HK SMEs plan new-market expansion within 2–3 years. SG MRA grant explicitly covers overseas market entry diagnostic. The Pro tier (USD 7k / SGD 9.5k) lines up perfectly with the budget envelope of the pre-expansion diagnostic phase.

06

Surface the "100% rework guarantee" prominently in proposal

The fair-value gap is structurally an uncertainty problem. The rework guarantee is the asymmetric trust contract that resolves it. Currently buried in the pricing page FAQ; surface it on the proposal cover and the executive readout.
