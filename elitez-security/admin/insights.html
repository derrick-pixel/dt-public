Industry Overview — Singapore 2026

Singapore's physical security services market is approximately SGD 3.5 billion, growing 5-6% annually. The sector is governed by the Police Licensing & Regulatory Department (PLRD) under the Private Security Industry Act. Roughly 280 active agencies hold the Security Agency Licence; the top five agencies (Certis, Aetos, Soverus/Secura, Concorde, Metropolis) capture an estimated 55-60% of revenue.

Five structural shifts are reshaping the industry through 2028:

  • Progressive Wage Model (PWM 2024-2028) — security officer basic floor S$2,938/mo (Sep 2024) escalates annually to S$4,400/mo by 2028. Total client cost rises from ~S$3,400/officer/mo today toward S$4,800-5,200/mo by 2028 on a like-for-like manpower basis.
  • SACE assessment (mandatory 2026) — Security Agency Competency Evaluation introduces a graded compliance benchmark; failing agencies face renewal risk.
  • Security ITM 2025 refresh — explicit government push from manpower-heavy to tech-augmented security models, with Productivity Solutions Grant (PSG) co-funding for AI-CCTV, VMS, LPR, and command-centre integration.
  • Outcome-Based Contracting (OBC) adoption — replacing headcount procurement, especially in MNC and government tenders. Rewards agencies that deliver measurable security outcomes per S$ spent.
  • Ageing workforce + sub-PWM consolidation — median officer age >55, recruitment pipeline drying up. Sub-PWM-floor budget agencies face exit pressure as PWM Mark and SACE filter out non-compliant operators.

Willingness-to-Pay Analysis

High Sensitivity

MCST Committee

Cost is the #1 friction at the renewal vote. Will accept the same monthly contract value if technology absorbs the PWM hike. Will not accept a 12-18% rate increase on like-for-like manpower. Unlock: PWM 2024-2028 cost calculator + costed Hillington Green / Hillsta case studies presented at the committee meeting.

Medium Sensitivity

Building & Property Manager

Will pay 10-15% premium for single-vendor admin that consolidates manpower + CCTV + LPR + VMS + command-centre into one contract and one bill. Wants monthly incident reports they can pass to landlord. Unlock: bundled pricing with "consolidation savings" line item that the building owner can defend in the AR.

Low Sensitivity

MNC Plant / EHS Director

Least price-sensitive — budget is pre-approved by corporate procurement. What matters: can you survive the audit? SLA, compliance binder, training records, escalation matrix, ISO 45001 evidence. Will pay 10-15% above mid-market for vendor that delivers a full audit pack within 5 working days of request.

High Sensitivity

Religious Site Trustee

Hard ceiling on monthly cost (set by parish council / management board). Will pay only if you can show net manpower-cost reduction with maintained or improved coverage. Auto Gate + LPR + Remote Command @ $4-5k/mo displaces $7-9k/mo manpower; net trustee win, easy council approval.

High Sensitivity

Eldercare Operator

Squeezed by MOH funding floors. Cannot afford an incident on safeguarding (board removal risk) but cannot afford 2x night officers either. Will pay for tech that reduces manpower while passing audit: virtual patrol + facial recognition + intercom replacing one night officer at ~$1,200/mo savings is exactly the wedge.

Competitive Landscape

Next-Best Alternatives

What prospects use instead of hiring a security agency — and how to position against each.

High Threat

Sub-PWM Budget Agencies

Tier-3/4 agencies pricing officers below S$3,200/mo, often by underpaying PWM benchmarks. Lowest common denominator; high attrition; chronic AWOL. Position against: "Won't survive PWM Mark + SACE 2026 audits. Your renewal vote becomes their exit notice."

Medium Threat

DIY CCTV (Ring / Reolink / consumer kit)

Small businesses and budget-strapped MCSTs install consumer cameras without monitoring. Position against: "Consumer cameras are evidence-collection devices, not deterrence. Our 24/7 command centre at Kallang means someone is watching and dispatching, not just recording."

High Threat

Auxiliary Police (Certis / Aetos / CISCO)

For high-security sites requiring armed officers, government tenders, airports. Elitez does not hold an auxiliary police licence. Position: complement rather than compete — "We handle PWM-compliant unarmed perimeter, MCST, and commercial work; they handle armed and government."

Medium Threat

In-House Security Team

Large MCSTs and MNCs sometimes hire officers directly. Higher control but higher total cost (CPF, leave, training, supervisor cover, audit binder). Position against: "Total cost of in-house is 30-40% higher once you factor compliance, training records, and disciplinary process. We provide all that under one contract."

Medium Threat

Facilities Management Bundlers

FM/IFM operators (ISS, Ramky, Concorde, Cushman & Wakefield) offering security as a low-priority add-on to cleaning/M&E. Security is not their core competency. Position against: "When your FM vendor handles security, it's the lowest-priority service in their book — and the first to be sub-contracted."

Low Threat

No Security (Status Quo)

Some sites operate without dedicated security, relying on locks, cameras, and luck. Position against: "Insurance premiums, liability exposure, and regulatory risk. One safeguarding incident costs more than a year of professional security."
